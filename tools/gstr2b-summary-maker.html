How to use it
STEP 01

Download GSTR-2B

On the GST portal → Returns → GSTR-2B, pick the period and use Download → Generate Excel. Repeat for every month you want to include.

STEP 02

Add the files

Drag the downloaded .xlsx files onto the Analyze tab, or choose them. Add multiple months together — the tool merges them.

STEP 03

Process

Click Process files. Every section — B2B, CDNR, ECO, ISD, amendments and ITC-reversal sheets — is read and normalised automatically.

STEP 04

Review & export

Check the party-wise ITC, records and alerts, then Download the combined Excel for your working papers or reconciliation.

What it does
  • Party-wise ITC summaryEvery supplier's taxable value + IGST/CGST/SGST/Cess totalled across all sections and months.
  • Rate-wise tax breakupFilter records by GST rate to reconcile 5% / 12% / 18% / 28% purchases quickly.
  • ITC eligibility flagsReads the portal's "ITC Available" column and separates eligible from ineligible credit.
  • Credit & debit notes handledCredit notes are auto-subtracted so your net ITC is correct, not double-counted.
  • Duplicate detectionSame GSTIN + invoice no + value across files is flagged so you don't claim twice.
  • Reverse-charge & POS alertsRCM supplies and negative/mismatched entries are surfaced in the Checks tab.
  • Combined Excel exportOne workbook: party summary, all records, per-section sheets and a checks sheet.
  • 100% privateEverything runs locally in your browser. Your purchase data never leaves your device.
Use the original Excel from the GST portal (not a re-saved or edited copy). The tool matches the portal's exact sheet names and two-row headers; a modified file may not parse.

About this tool. The GSTR-2B Analyzer reads the official Excel exported from the GST portal and totals it for your convenience. ITC eligibility shown here reflects the "ITC Available" flag in the portal file; final eligibility still depends on Section 16 conditions (goods/services received, tax actually paid by the supplier, and Rule 42/43 and Section 17(5) restrictions) which this tool cannot verify. Credit notes are treated as reductions to net credit. Always reconcile against your purchase register and books before claiming ITC in GSTR-3B. This tool is general guidance, not professional advice.
