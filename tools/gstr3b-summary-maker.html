How to use it
STEP 01

Download GSTR-3B

On the GST portal → Returns → GSTR-3B, open each filed month and use Download filed GSTR-3B (PDF). Save every month you want in the report.

STEP 02

Add the PDFs

Drag the downloaded PDFs onto the Upload tab, or choose them. Add the full April→March set for a complete year — any missing month stays blank.

STEP 03

Generate

Click Generate summary. Each PDF's period is detected and its figures are read for outward supply, ITC, RCM and late fee & interest.

STEP 04

Review & export

Check the annual summary, tax-vs-cash and compliance tabs. Fill the editable opening-balance and portal-ledger fields, then export to Excel, CSV or PDF.

What it does
  • Full-year consolidationTwelve monthly 3Bs merged into one month-wise grid with automatic totals.
  • Outward, ITC & RCMReads Table 3.1 outward (incl. zero-rated tax), Table 4 net ITC and 3.1(d) reverse charge.
  • Tax-vs-cash workingOutput less ITC gives net liability; RCM, late fee and interest are added to show cash outflow.
  • Compliance snapshotFlags months with late fee or interest, and separates RCM liability for the year.
  • Editable ledger fieldsOpening balance and the portal credit ledger aren't in the 3B — enter them and the closing balance updates live.
  • Excel · CSV · PDF exportTake the whole report into your working papers or a client-ready PDF in one click.
  • GSTR-9 groundworkThe consolidated year is the starting base for annual-return reconciliation.
  • 100% privatePDFs are parsed in the browser with PDF.js. Nothing is uploaded to any server.
Use the text-based PDF filed on the portal, not a scanned or photographed copy. Scanned images have no text layer, so figures can't be read. If a month shows "no figures", open that PDF and confirm you can select its text.

About this tool. Figures are extracted only from the GSTR-3B PDFs you upload. Fields that a GSTR-3B does not contain — opening balances and the electronic cash/credit ledger from the portal — are shown as editable or N/A. The tax-vs-cash working assumes ITC is set off against output in the usual IGST→CGST/SGST order; your actual utilisation on the portal may differ. RCM is always payable in cash and cannot be paid from ITC. Rounding follows the returns as filed. This is general guidance for reconciliation, not professional advice — verify against the original returns and the electronic ledgers before relying on it.
