Incorporating a company in India is done entirely online through the **MCA V3 portal** using the **SPICe+** web form. The process itself is straightforward — the single most common reason incorporations get delayed is incomplete or mismatched paperwork. This guide gives you the complete checklist of every detail and document you need **before** you start: relating to the **company**, to **directors who are Indian nationals**, and to **directors who are foreign nationals** — along with the accepted file format and size for each document.

### 1. What is a Company? (In Short)

A company is a legal entity incorporated under the **Companies Act, 2013** and registered with the Registrar of Companies (RoC). Once incorporated, it has an identity separate from its owners — it can own property, open bank accounts, enter into contracts and sue or be sued in its own name. The liability of its members is limited to the amount unpaid on their shares, which is the single biggest reason businesses choose to incorporate. Every company receives a **Certificate of Incorporation (CoI)** with a unique CIN, and its PAN and TAN are allotted along with it.

<a id="coinc-2"></a>

### 2. Types of Company

| Type | Min. Directors | Min. Members | Best Suited For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Private Limited (Pvt Ltd)** | 2 | 2 shareholders | Startups, family businesses, companies raising investment |
| **One Person Company (OPC)** | 1 | 1 (+ 1 nominee) | Solo founders wanting limited liability |
| **Public Limited** | 3 | 7 shareholders | Large businesses raising capital from the public |
| **Section 8 Company** | 2 | 2 | Non-profit objects — charity, education, social welfare |
| **Producer Company** | 5 | 10 individuals / 2 institutions | Farmer and producer groups |
| **Nidhi Company** | 3 | 7 | Mutual benefit — borrowing/lending among members |

**Note:** At least **one director must be a resident of India** (stayed in India for 120 days or more during the financial year) — this applies even when all shareholders are foreign nationals or foreign companies.

### 3. File Format & Size Requirements on the MCA Portal

The incorporation is filed through **SPICe+** (Part A for name reservation, Part B for incorporation) along with the linked forms **e-MoA (INC-33)**, **e-AoA (INC-34)**, **AGILE-PRO-S (INC-35)** and **INC-9**. Before scanning anything, keep these limits in mind:

| Requirement | Limit |
|---|---|
| Accepted formats | **PDF or JPG** only |
| Maximum size | Total form with all attachments up to **10 MB** |
| Practical per-document size | Keep each scanned document within **2 MB** |
| Scan quality | Black & white, 200–300 DPI — clear and complete |
| Attestation | Every copy **self-attested**; foreign documents notarised / apostilled / consularised |

### 4. Documents & Details Related to the Company

**Details to be decided/provided:**

1. **Proposed names** — at least 2 name options in order of preference (SPICe+ Part A allows 2 names; keep a third ready in case of resubmission)
2. **Main objects / nature of business** — what the company will actually do, with the matching **NIC code**
3. **Authorised capital** — e.g. ₹1,00,000 (no minimum capital requirement, but fees and stamp duty depend on it)
4. **Paid-up capital** and **shareholding pattern** — number of shares and percentage of each subscriber
5. **Registered office address** — full address with PIN code and police station jurisdiction
6. **Company email ID and mobile number** — must be accessible for OTP verification
7. **Preferred bank** for the company's current account (opened through AGILE-PRO-S)

**Documents to be attached:**

| Document | Format & Size |
|---|---|
| Utility bill of registered office — electricity / telephone / gas / water bill, **not older than 2 months** | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| **NOC (No Objection Certificate)** from the owner of the premises | PDF, up to 2 MB |
| **Rent agreement / lease deed** (if rented) or **sale deed / property papers** (if owned) | PDF, up to 2 MB |
| **Trademark / NOC** if the proposed name resembles a registered trademark or an existing company (with board resolution, where applicable) | PDF, up to 2 MB |

**Tip:** The landlord's name on the electricity bill and on the rent agreement/NOC must match exactly — a mismatch here is one of the most common reasons for resubmission.

<a id="coinc-5"></a>

### 5. Documents & Details — Directors (Indian Nationals)

**Details for each director/subscriber:**

1. Full name (exactly as per PAN), father's name, date of birth, place of birth
2. Nationality and **DIN** (only if already allotted — up to 3 new DINs are allotted through SPICe+ itself)
3. Educational qualification and occupation
4. Current residential address, permanent address (if different), and **duration of stay** at the current address
5. Personal **email ID and mobile number** (unique for each director — OTP verification is done on these)
6. Number of shares / shareholding percentage
7. Whether the person is also a **subscriber to the MoA**

**Documents for each director/subscriber:**

| Document | Format & Size |
|---|---|
| **PAN card** (mandatory identity proof for Indian nationals) | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| **Aadhaar card** | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| Additional ID proof — **Voter ID / Passport / Driving Licence** (any one) | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| Residential address proof — **bank statement / electricity / telephone / mobile bill**, **not older than 2 months** | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| **Passport-size photograph** (white background) | JPG, 50–100 KB |
| **Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)** — Class 3, for all subscribers and directors signing the forms | — |
| **DIR-2** — consent to act as director | PDF, up to 2 MB |
| **INC-9** — declaration by subscribers and first directors | Auto-generated |

**Critical:** Name, father's name and date of birth must match **exactly** between PAN and Aadhaar. Any mismatch stalls the DSC application as well as the incorporation — get it corrected first.

<a id="coinc-6"></a>

### 6. Documents & Details — Directors (Foreign Nationals / NRIs)

Same personal details as Indian directors (name, father's name, date of birth, nationality, occupation, address, email, mobile, shareholding), plus **passport number** and country of issue.

**Documents for each foreign director/subscriber:**

| Document | Format & Size |
|---|---|
| **Passport** (mandatory identity proof — notarised/apostilled) | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| Address proof — **driving licence / bank statement / government-issued residence card / utility bill**, not older than 2 months | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| **Passport-size photograph** (white background) | JPG, 50–100 KB |
| **PAN** — not applicable; a declaration of "no PAN" is given instead (NRIs holding Indian PAN must provide it) | PDF |
| **Business visa** — if the foreign national is present in India and signing documents here | PDF/JPG, up to 2 MB |
| **DSC** — Class 3, on the basis of the attested passport and address proof | — |
| **DIR-2** — consent to act as director (attested in the same manner as other documents) | PDF, up to 2 MB |

**Attestation rules for foreign documents (very important):**

- **Country in the Commonwealth** → documents notarised by a Notary Public of that country
- **Country party to the Hague Apostille Convention** → documents notarised and **apostilled** in that country
- **Any other country** → documents attested by the **Indian Embassy/Consulate** in that country (consularisation)
- If the foreign national is in India on a valid **business visa**, documents can be notarised in India instead — but the visa copy must be attached, otherwise the form is liable to be rejected without resubmission

<a id="coinc-7"></a>

### 7. If the Signatory is an Authorised Person / Representative

Where a body corporate is a subscriber, or an authorised representative signs on behalf of subscribers, keep ready:

1. Name and details of the authorised person
2. **Board resolution / authorisation letter** authorising the person (PDF, up to 2 MB)
3. Passport-size photograph (JPG, 50–100 KB) and **specimen signature** (JPG/PDF) — also needed for EPFO registration through AGILE-PRO-S

<a id="coinc-8"></a>

### 8. Important Points to Take Care Of

- **PAN–Aadhaar match** — name, father's name and date of birth should match between PAN and Aadhaar for every Indian director.
- **Fresh address proofs** — address proof of directors and the utility bill of the registered office should not be older than **2 months** on the date of filing.
- **Landlord consistency** — the landlord's name and address must match between the electricity bill, the rent agreement and the NOC.
- **Self-attestation** — every copy must be self-attested; foreign nationals' documents additionally notarised / apostilled / consularised.
- **Unique contact details** — each director should have a personal email and mobile number; OTPs are sent for verification and shared/duplicate contacts create problems.
- **Address from a valid proof** — the residential address entered in the form should be picked from the address proof being attached, word for word.
- **File hygiene** — scan in black & white, 200–300 DPI, PDF/JPG only, each document within 2 MB (form total limit: 10 MB).

<a id="coinc-9"></a>

### 9. What You Receive After Incorporation

Once the checklist is complete, SPICe+ Part A (name reservation) and Part B are filed along with e-MoA, e-AoA, AGILE-PRO-S and INC-9. On approval, you receive in one go:

- **Certificate of Incorporation** with CIN, **PAN and TAN**
- **DINs** for directors who didn't already have one
- **EPFO, ESIC and professional tax** registrations (where applicable)
- Company **bank account** opening initiated through AGILE-PRO-S
- **GST registration**, if opted for in the same form

### 10. FAQs

**Is there any minimum capital required to incorporate a company?**
No. There is no minimum capital requirement — you can incorporate with any authorised capital (even ₹10,000), though government fees and stamp duty are calculated on it.

**Can a company be registered at a residential address?**
Yes. A home address can be the registered office, with the utility bill and an NOC/consent from the owner of the premises.

**Is physically visiting any office required?**
No. The entire process — name reservation, incorporation, PAN, TAN, and even bank account initiation — is completely online through the MCA portal.

**How many DINs can be allotted through SPICe+?**
Up to 3 new DINs are allotted through SPICe+ itself. Directors who already hold a DIN simply quote it.

**Do foreign directors need an Indian PAN?**
No. Foreign nationals without an Indian PAN give a "no PAN" declaration. NRIs who already hold an Indian PAN must provide it.

**How long does incorporation take?**
With a complete, correctly attested set of documents, incorporation is typically approved within about 3–7 working days of filing.

### Key Takeaways

- Gather everything **before** filing — company proofs, director KYC and attested foreign documents — using the checklist above.
- Follow the MCA limits to the letter: **PDF/JPG only, form total within 10 MB**, each document within 2 MB.
- Match every name, date and address exactly with PAN/Aadhaar and the attached proofs — mismatches are the #1 cause of resubmission.
- Address proofs (director and registered office) must be **less than 2 months old**.
- Foreign documents must be **notarised / apostilled / consularised** as per the country — never plain photocopies.
