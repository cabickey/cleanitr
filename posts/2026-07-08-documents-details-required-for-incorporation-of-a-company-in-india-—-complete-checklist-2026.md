Incorporating a company in India is done entirely online through the MCA V3 portal using the SPICe+ web form. The process itself is straightforward — what delays most incorporations is incomplete or mismatched paperwork. This checklist covers every detail and document you need before you start: relating to the company, to directors who are Indian nationals, and to directors who are foreign nationals — along with the accepted file format and size for each document.

What is a Company? (In Short)

A company is a legal entity incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013 and registered with the Registrar of Companies (RoC). Once incorporated, it has an identity separate from its owners — it can own property, open bank accounts, enter into contracts and sue or be sued in its own name. The liability of its members is limited to the amount unpaid on their shares, which is the single biggest reason businesses choose to incorporate. Every company receives a Certificate of Incorporation (CoI) with a unique CIN, and its PAN and TAN are allotted along with it.

Documents & Details Checklist

All documents are uploaded as attachments to SPICe+ and its linked forms. Accepted formats: PDF or JPG. The total size of the form with all attachments must not exceed 10 MB — so scan documents in black & white at around 200–300 DPI and keep each file within 2 MB. All copies must be self-attested.

A. Related to the Company

Details to be provided:


Proposed names — at least 2 options in order of preference (keep a third ready in case of resubmission)
Main objects / nature of business — with the matching NIC code
Authorised capital — e.g. ₹1,00,000 (no minimum requirement, but fees and stamp duty depend on it)
Paid-up capital and shareholding pattern — shares and percentage of each subscriber
Registered office address — full address with PIN code and police station jurisdiction
Company email ID and mobile number — must be accessible for OTP verification
Preferred bank for the company's current account (opened through AGILE-PRO-S)


Documents to be attached:

Tip: The landlord's name on the electricity bill and on the rent agreement/NOC must match exactly — a mismatch here is one of the most common reasons for resubmission.



B. Related to Directors — Indian Nationals

Details for each director/subscriber:


Full name (exactly as per PAN), father's name, date of birth, place of birth
Nationality and DIN (only if already allotted — up to 3 new DINs are allotted through SPICe+ itself)
Educational qualification and occupation
Current residential address, permanent address (if different), and duration of stay at the current address
Personal email ID and mobile number (unique for each director — used for OTP verification)
Number of shares / shareholding percentage
Whether the person is also a subscriber to the MoA

Attestation rules for foreign documents (very important):


Commonwealth country → documents notarised by a Notary Public of that country
Hague Apostille Convention country → documents notarised and apostilled in that country
Any other country → attested by the Indian Embassy/Consulate there (consularisation)
Foreign national in India on a valid business visa → documents can be notarised in India, but the visa copy must be attached — otherwise the form is liable to be rejected without resubmission


If the Signatory is an Authorised Person / Representative

Where a body corporate is a subscriber, or an authorised representative signs on behalf of subscribers, keep ready:


Name and details of the authorised person
Board resolution / authorisation letter (PDF, up to 2 MB)
Passport-size photograph (JPG, 50–100 KB) and specimen signature (JPG/PDF) — also needed for EPFO registration through AGILE-PRO-S


Important Points to Take Care Of


PAN–Aadhaar match: Name, father's name and date of birth should match between PAN and Aadhaar for every Indian director.
Fresh address proofs: Address proof of directors and the utility bill of the registered office should not be older than 2 months on the date of filing.
Landlord consistency: The landlord's name and address must match between the electricity bill, the rent agreement and the NOC.
Self-attestation: Every copy must be self-attested; foreign nationals' documents additionally notarised / apostilled / consularised.
Unique contact details: Each director should have a personal email and mobile number — OTPs are sent for verification.
Address from a valid proof: The residential address in the form should be picked from the attached address proof, word for word.
File hygiene: Scan in black & white, 200–300 DPI, PDF/JPG only, each document within 2 MB (form total limit: 10 MB).


What Happens After You Submit the Documents

Once the checklist is complete, we file SPICe+ Part A (name reservation) and Part B along with e-MoA, e-AoA, AGILE-PRO-S and INC-9. On approval, you receive in one go: the Certificate of Incorporation with CIN, PAN and TAN, DINs for new directors, and registrations for EPFO, ESIC and professional tax (where applicable), along with the company bank account opening initiated through AGILE-PRO-S. GST registration can also be opted for in the same form.
