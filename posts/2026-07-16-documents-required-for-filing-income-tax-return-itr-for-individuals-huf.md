Filing an accurate Income Tax Return begins with keeping the right documents ready. Missing or incorrect information is the single most common reason for **mismatches, defective returns, delayed refunds and notices** from the department. This guide lists exactly what you need for **FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)**, organised by category, along with the ITR forms applicable to Individuals and HUFs, the deductions you can claim, and the relevant due dates.

> **Applicable law:** This checklist is for **FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)** returns, which are governed entirely by the **Income-tax Act, 1961**. The new Income Tax Act, 2025 does **not** apply to this year — it applies only to income earned from 1 April 2026 (Tax Year 2026-27, filed in 2027).

### 1. Basic / Common Documents (Required for Everyone)

These are needed regardless of your income type or which ITR form applies:

1. **PAN card**
2. **Aadhaar card** — mandatory to be linked with PAN. From AY 2026-27, the Aadhaar **number** is required; the Aadhaar Enrolment ID is no longer accepted.
3. **Bank account details** — account number and IFSC of all active accounts (one selected for refund).
4. **Form 26AS** — consolidated tax statement (TDS/TCS, advance tax, self-assessment tax).
5. **Annual Information Statement (AIS) & Taxpayer Information Summary (TIS)** — downloadable from the e-filing portal.
6. **Login credentials** for the income tax e-filing portal.
7. **Previous year's ITR** — for reference and carry-forward of losses.

### 2. Income-Specific Documents

#### A. Salary Income

- **Form 16** (Parts A & B) from the employer
- Salary slips (if needed for allowance/perquisite details)
- Pension statement (for pensioners)

#### B. House Property Income

*(Interest deduction is claimed under Section 24(b).)*

- **Home loan interest certificate** from the bank/lender
- Municipal / property tax payment receipts
- Rent received details / rent agreement (for let-out property); and the **amount of unrealised rent**, if any (a dedicated field has been added this year)
- Co-owner details and share, if jointly owned

#### C. Capital Gains

- Broker statement / capital gains statement for shares and mutual funds
- Sale and purchase deeds for property
- Details of cost of acquisition, improvement, and transfer expenses
- For FY 2025-26, a **uniform LTCG rate of 12.5%** applies for the full year — the pre/post 23 July 2024 rate split has been **removed** from the ITR forms this year. LTCG on listed equity / equity mutual funds is **exempt up to ₹1.25 lakh**, and taxed at 12.5% beyond that.
- For **immovable property purchased before 23 July 2024** and sold on or after that date: a grandfathering option applies — **lower of 20% with indexation or 12.5% without** (keep your indexed-cost workings).

#### D. Business / Profession Income

- Books of account, profit & loss statement, and balance sheet
- Bank statements of business accounts
- GST returns (if registered)
- Details of gross receipts / turnover (for presumptive taxation under Sections 44AD / 44ADA / 44AE)
- Tax audit report (Form 3CA/3CB/3CD), where applicable

#### E. Income from Other Sources

- Interest certificates from banks and post office (savings, FD, RD)
- Dividend income statements
- Details of interest on income tax refund
- Documents for any other income (gifts, family pension, winnings, etc.)

### 3. Deduction & Exemption Proofs (Chapter VI-A and others)

Retain these to claim deductions, especially under the **Old Tax Regime**:

| Section | Deduction | Keep these proofs |
|---|---|---|
| **80C** | LIC, PPF, ELSS, tuition fees, home loan principal, 5-yr FD | Policy / account number, insurer or institution name, amount, receipt date |
| **80D** | Health insurance premium | Insurer name, policy number, premium, preventive health check-up |
| **80E** | Education loan interest | Lender name, loan account number, interest certificate |
| **80G** | Donations | Donee name & PAN, amount, **bank IFSC code and transaction/UTR reference number** (now required) |
| **80TTA / 80TTB** | Savings / FD interest | Bank-wise interest figures |
| **80CCD(1B)** | Additional NPS (₹50,000) | PRAN, contribution proof |
| — | HRA / LTA | Rent receipts, rent agreement, travel proofs (if claimed) |

> **Note:** Under the **New Tax Regime** (default from AY 2024-25 under **Section 115BAC**), most Chapter VI-A deductions are **not** available. Keep these proofs only if you are opting for the Old Regime. To opt out of the New Regime, **Form 10-IEA** must be filed.

### 4. Tax Payment Documents

- **TDS certificates** — Form 16A for non-salary TDS
- **Advance tax** challans
- **Self-assessment tax** challans

### 5. Which ITR Form Applies to You?

| Form | Who Can File | Key Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **ITR-1 (Sahaj)** | Resident Individual only (not HUF) | Total income up to ₹50 lakh; income from salary/pension, **up to two house properties**, other sources, agricultural income up to ₹5,000. LTCG u/s 112A up to ₹1.25 lakh permitted, with no carry-forward losses |
| **ITR-2** | Individuals & HUF | No business/profession income; includes capital gains, more than one house property, foreign income/assets, income above ₹50 lakh |
| **ITR-3** | Individuals & HUF | Income from business or profession (including partners drawing salary/interest from a firm) |
| **ITR-4 (Sugam)** | Resident Individual, HUF & Firms (other than LLP) | Presumptive income under Sections 44AD, 44ADA, 44AE; total income up to ₹50 lakh; **up to two house properties**. LTCG u/s 112A up to ₹1.25 lakh permitted |

> **Important:** From AY 2026-27, ITR-1 and ITR-4 can be used even with **two house properties**. However, they still **cannot** be used by a person who is a company director, holds unlisted equity shares, has foreign assets, has any short-term capital gains, has LTCG u/s 112A above ₹1.25 lakh, or has any carried-forward losses.

### 6. Due Dates for Filing ITR – FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)

| Category | Due Date |
|---|---|
| Individuals/HUF filing **ITR-1 or ITR-2** (non-audit) | **31 July 2026** |
| Individuals/HUF filing **ITR-3 or ITR-4** (non-audit) | **31 August 2026** |
| Taxpayers subject to tax audit (Sec 44AB) | 31 October 2026 |
| Taxpayers with transfer pricing report (Form 3CEB) | 30 November 2026 |
| **Belated Return** (Sec 139(4)) | 31 December 2026 |
| **Revised Return** (Sec 139(5)) — *extended by Budget 2026* | **31 March 2027** |
| **Updated Return** (ITR-U, Sec 139(8A)) | Within 48 months from end of AY (by 31 March 2031) |

*These dates apply unless extended by the CBDT. As of now, no extension has been notified for AY 2026-27; any future extension will be announced by the Income Tax Department.*

### 7. Consequences of Late Filing

- **Late filing fee under Section 234F:** up to **₹5,000**; reduced to **₹1,000** if total income is below ₹5 lakh.
- **Interest under Section 234A:** 1% per month (or part of a month) on unpaid tax.
- **Loss of carry-forward:** most business losses and capital losses cannot be carried forward if the return is filed after the due date.
- **Delayed refunds:** late returns move to the back of the processing queue.

### 8. FAQs

**Which documents does every taxpayer need, no matter what?**
PAN, Aadhaar, bank details, Form 26AS, and AIS/TIS. Everything else depends on your income sources and the deductions you claim.

**Do I need investment proofs under the New Tax Regime?**
Mostly no. The New Regime (Section 115BAC) disallows most Chapter VI-A deductions, so investment proofs matter mainly if you opt for the Old Regime using Form 10-IEA.

**Can I file ITR-1 if I own two houses?**
Yes — from AY 2026-27, ITR-1 (and ITR-4) allow up to two house properties, provided all other eligibility conditions are met.

**Is the 23 July 2024 capital-gains split still required this year?**
No. For FY 2025-26 the full-year uniform 12.5% LTCG rate applies, and the separate pre/post 23 July 2024 reporting has been removed from the forms.

**By when can I revise my return for FY 2025-26?**
The revised-return deadline has been extended to **31 March 2027** (Section 139(5)). A belated return can be filed up to 31 December 2026.

**Should I reconcile before filing?**
Always. Matching your figures with **Form 26AS and AIS** before filing is the single most effective step to avoid notices.

### Key Takeaways

- Keep the **common set** — PAN, Aadhaar, bank details, Form 26AS, AIS/TIS — ready before you start.
- Gather **income-specific documents** based on your salary, house property, capital gains, business and other income.
- Keep **deduction proofs** only if you're on the Old Regime; the New Regime needs very few.
- Pick the **right ITR form** — using the wrong one makes the return defective under Section 139(9).
- File by **31 July / 31 August 2026** to avoid penalties and protect your loss carry-forward.
